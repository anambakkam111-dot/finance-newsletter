Learning Topics

Vocab Vault

Core financial terms for beginners and advanced student readers.

APR

Annual percentage rate; yearly interest charged for credit use.

Asset

Something with economic value that can produce return.

Bond

Debt instrument that pays interest and returns principal over time.

Budget

A plan for managing income, spending, and saving.

Capital Gains

Profit earned when an investment is sold for more than cost basis.

Checking Account

Bank account used for frequent deposits, withdrawals, and payments.

Compound Interest

Interest earned on both principal and previously earned interest.

Credit Report

Record showing credit accounts, balances, and payment history.

Credit Score

Numeric rating of a borrower’s repayment reliability.

Diversification

Spreading investments to reduce concentration risk.

Dividend

Cash distribution from a company’s profits to shareholders.

Emergency Fund

Cash reserve set aside for unexpected expenses.

ETF

Exchange-traded fund holding a basket of securities.

FICO Score

Common credit scoring model used by lenders.

Financial Literacy

Ability to understand and apply money concepts in daily decisions.

Fixed Expenses

Costs that stay about the same each month.

Grace Period

Time before finance charges apply to certain balances.

Human Capital

Skills and knowledge that increase earning ability.

Inflation

Broad rise in prices that reduces purchasing power over time.

Inflation Risk

Risk that prices rise faster than your investment returns.

Installment Plan

Loan repaid in fixed periodic payments over time.

Interest

Cost of borrowing money or reward for saving it.

Liquidity

How quickly an asset can be turned into cash.

Liquidity Risk

Risk of not being able to sell an asset quickly at fair value.

Liability

Debt or financial obligation owed to another party.

Long-Term Goal

Financial goal targeted for more than five years out.

Money Market Account

Interest-bearing account with limited transaction features.

Mutual Fund

Pooled investment vehicle managed by professionals.

Net Income

Income left after taxes and necessary deductions.

Net Worth

Total assets minus total liabilities.

Opportunity Cost

Value of the best alternative given up by a choice.

Open-Ended Credit

Reusable credit line with flexible borrowing and repayment.

Rate of Return

Percentage gain or loss on an investment over a period.

Revolving Credit

Credit account that can be borrowed repeatedly to a limit.

Risk

Chance that an investment outcome differs from expectations.

Rule of 72

Quick estimate for doubling time: 72 ÷ annual return rate.

Savings Account

Interest-bearing deposit account for storing cash.

Scarcity

Condition where limited resources cannot satisfy all wants.

Short-Term Goal

Goal expected to be reached in under one year.

Stock

Ownership share in a corporation.

Taxes

Required payments to government authorities.

Trade-Off

What you give up to get something else.

Variable Expenses

Spending that can change week-to-week or month-to-month.
