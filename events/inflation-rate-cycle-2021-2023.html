Inflation & Rate Cycle (2021–2023)

After pandemic distortions and supply-demand imbalances, inflation accelerated in many economies. Central banks responded with one of the fastest tightening cycles in decades, raising policy rates to restrain price pressures.

Higher rates affected borrowing costs, valuation multiples, housing affordability, and corporate financing conditions. Market leadership shifted repeatedly as investors repriced growth, duration, and credit risk.

Student takeaway: macro variables can materially influence every asset class. Build a habit of connecting company-level analysis with interest-rate and inflation dynamics.
