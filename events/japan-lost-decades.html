Japan’s Lost Decades

After large asset bubbles in equities and real estate burst in the early 1990s, Japan entered a prolonged period of subdued growth and recurring deflation pressure. Balance-sheet repair across households, firms, and banks took many years, and confidence remained weak for long stretches.

The macro backdrop challenged policymakers: low rates and repeated stimulus did not always translate into strong private demand. Markets and economists studied Japan closely to understand debt overhangs, demographic effects, and the limits of conventional policy tools.

Student takeaway: recoveries are not always fast, and country concentration can be costly. A globally diversified portfolio can reduce the impact of long domestic stagnation cycles.
