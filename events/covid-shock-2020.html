COVID-19 Shock (2020)

In early 2020, global markets dropped rapidly as pandemic-related shutdowns disrupted production, services, and mobility. Uncertainty around public health outcomes and policy responses drove extraordinary volatility across asset classes.

Massive monetary and fiscal support helped stabilize conditions, and many risk assets rebounded quickly, though impacts varied significantly across industries and income groups. The period underscored how exogenous shocks can alter economic trajectories almost overnight.

Student takeaway: keep a long-term plan and avoid emotional trading during headlines. Crisis periods often reward disciplined risk management and consistent investing behavior.
