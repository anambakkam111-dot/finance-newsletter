Black Tuesday (1929)

Black Tuesday on October 29, 1929 is widely remembered as the climax of a violent stock-market collapse in the United States. Heavy selling pressure overwhelmed demand and forced liquidation accelerated losses.

The crash helped tighten financial conditions and amplified credit fragility during the broader Depression era. Bank failures and reduced lending capacity deepened real-economy weakness.

Valuation discipline, diversification, and careful leverage management remain core lessons from this period.
