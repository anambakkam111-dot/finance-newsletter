Black Monday (1987)

On October 19, 1987, major equity indices dropped sharply in one trading day, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average falling over 20%. The decline reflected a mix of fragile sentiment, portfolio insurance mechanics, and liquidity stress in rapidly moving markets.

The event accelerated discussions about market structure and led to stronger safeguards, including circuit breakers. It also highlighted the speed with which feedback loops can magnify downside moves when many participants attempt to hedge or exit simultaneously.

For students, Black Monday is a case study in liquidity risk and market microstructure. Risk planning should include "what if" scenarios for sudden gaps, not only gradual declines.
