Global Financial Crisis (2008)

The 2008 crisis emerged from housing-market weakness, complex securitization risks, and heavy leverage across financial institutions. As defaults rose and confidence dropped, funding markets froze and losses spread quickly through interconnected balance sheets.

Governments and central banks responded with emergency liquidity tools, recapitalization programs, and eventually tighter regulatory frameworks. The episode reshaped global banking oversight and elevated systemic-risk monitoring as a policy priority.

Student takeaway: understanding leverage, counterparty risk, and credit quality is essential. Diversification and cash-flow resilience matter most when market stress is broad and persistent.
