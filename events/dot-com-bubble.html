Dot-Com Bubble (1995–2002)

During the late 1990s, internet-related companies attracted intense investor attention and soaring valuations. Many firms had compelling narratives but limited earnings visibility, and valuation frameworks often depended on optimistic growth assumptions.

When expectations reset, many stocks fell sharply, and capital rotated toward businesses with stronger fundamentals. The period showed that technological transformation can be real while individual security pricing can still become detached from sustainable cash flows.

Student takeaway: innovation matters, but price paid matters too. Use valuation discipline and verify unit economics before buying into fast-moving themes.
